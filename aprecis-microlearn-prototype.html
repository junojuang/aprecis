Why this works — what Mimo gets right

Mimo's retention secret isn't gamification — it's that reading is never the primary action. You tap, you guess, you type, you rearrange. Aprecis v2 applies this to AI research:

  • One paper per day, not a feed. Removes decision fatigue (the kind that drives doomscrolling in the first place).
  • 6 cards · 2 minutes · <40 words per screen. Hard ceiling — if a concept won't fit, split it.
  • Every card has an interaction. Tap-to-reveal, fill-blank, MCQ, drag-to-sort, or tap-on-diagram. No passive reading screens.
  • XP + streak + level. Return driver. Not a trick — it's the difference between "I should read more papers" and actually doing it.
  • The serif italic takeaway. The single sentence that survives the deck. Shown at the end, not the beginning — so it lands, not prefaces.
  • arXiv link is the reward. Flip the hierarchy: the paper unlocks after you've understood the shape of the idea, not as the starting point.
