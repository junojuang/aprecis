Era 1: Foundations 1943 to 1985

1.A Logical Calculus of Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity
McCulloch and Pitts, 1943
First mathematical model of a neuron. Proved that networks of artificial neurons could compute logical functions. The seed of every neural network that followed.
2.Computing Machinery and Intelligence
Turing, 1950
Defined the question (can machines think?) and the Turing Test. Set the field's north star for 70 years.
3.The Perceptron: A Probabilistic Model for Information Storage and Organization
Rosenblatt, 1958
First learning machine. Showed that weights could be adjusted automatically from data. The press called it "the embryo of an electronic computer that thinks."
4.Some Studies in Machine Learning Using the Game of Checkers
Samuel, 1959
First program to learn from self-play. Coined the term "machine learning."
5.Perceptrons: An Introduction to Computational Geometry
Minsky and Papert, 1969
Proved perceptrons cannot learn XOR. Killed neural-network funding and triggered the first AI winter. Cultural villain, vindicated 30 years later.
6.Beyond Regression: New Tools for Prediction and Analysis in the Behavioral Sciences
Werbos, 1974 (PhD thesis)
Invented backpropagation. Sat ignored for a decade.
7.Neural Networks and Physical Systems with Emergent Collective Computational Abilities
Hopfield, 1982
Showed networks could store memories like a physicist's system. Reignited mainstream interest in neural networks.
8.Learning Representations by Back-Propagating Errors
Rumelhart, Hinton, Williams, 1986
Popularized backpropagation. The shot heard around connectionism.

Era 2: Early Networks 1986 to 1999

9.Backpropagation Applied to Handwritten Zip Code Recognition
LeCun et al., 1989
First convolutional network. Postal automation became the first commercial neural-net deployment.
10.Finding Structure in Time
Elman, 1990
Simple recurrent network. Showed neural nets could handle sequences with memory.
11.Support-Vector Networks
Cortes and Vapnik, 1995
Support Vector Machines. Dominated machine learning for a decade and sidelined neural networks again.
12.Long Short-Term Memory
Hochreiter and Schmidhuber, 1997
Solved the vanishing gradient problem. Powered every recurrent net for 20 years until transformers arrived.
13.Gradient-Based Learning Applied to Document Recognition (LeNet-5)
LeCun, Bottou, Bengio, Haffner, 1998
Defined the modern CNN architecture: conv, pool, conv, pool, fully connected.
14.The Anatomy of a Large-Scale Hypertextual Web Search Engine
Brin and Page, 1998
PageRank. Showed machine learning at web scale. Built Google.
15.Random Forests
Breiman, 2001
Ensemble methods. Won every Kaggle competition for a decade.
16.Greedy Function Approximation: A Gradient Boosting Machine
Friedman, 2001
Gradient boosting. Foundation of XGBoost. Still the king of tabular data.
17.A Neural Probabilistic Language Model
Bengio et al., 2003
First neural language model. Direct ancestor of GPT.
18.Reducing the Dimensionality of Data with Neural Networks
Hinton and Salakhutdinov, 2006
Deep Belief Networks. First time "deep" learning actually worked. Started the deep-learning revival.

Era 3: Pre-Deep-Learning Groundwork 2001 to 2014

19.ImageNet: A Large-Scale Hierarchical Image Database
Deng, Dong, Socher, Li, Li, Fei-Fei, 2009
The dataset that enabled everything. No ImageNet, no AlexNet, no deep-learning boom. Datasets matter more than algorithms.
20.ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (AlexNet)
Krizhevsky, Sutskever, Hinton, 2012
The big-bang moment. A 10-point error reduction on ImageNet shocked the field. GPUs plus data plus depth equals revolution.
21.Improving Neural Networks by Preventing Co-adaptation of Feature Detectors (Dropout)
Hinton et al., 2012
Regularization that finally let deep networks generalize instead of overfitting.
22.Efficient Estimation of Word Representations in Vector Space (Word2Vec)
Mikolov et al., 2013
Words as vectors. "King minus Man plus Woman equals Queen." Defined modern NLP.
23.Adam: A Method for Stochastic Optimization
Kingma and Ba, 2014
The optimizer everyone still uses. One of the most cited papers in modern machine learning.
24.Batch Normalization: Accelerating Deep Network Training by Reducing Internal Covariate Shift
Ioffe and Szegedy, 2015
Made very deep networks trainable. Standard ingredient in nearly every architecture after.
25.GloVe: Global Vectors for Word Representation
Pennington, Socher, Manning, 2014
Alternative to Word2Vec built from co-occurrence statistics. Widely used in production NLP.
26.Auto-Encoding Variational Bayes (VAE)
Kingma and Welling, 2013
Probabilistic generative models with latent variables. Foundation of all latent-space thinking.

Era 4: Deep Learning Explosion 2014 to 2017

27.Sequence to Sequence Learning with Neural Networks
Sutskever, Vinyals, Le, 2014
Encoder-decoder architecture. Defined modern translation, summarization, and dialogue systems.
28.Neural Machine Translation by Jointly Learning to Align and Translate
Bahdanau, Cho, Bengio, 2014
First attention mechanism. The conceptual seed of transformers.
29.Generative Adversarial Networks
Goodfellow et al., 2014
Two networks fighting. Launched the generative-modeling era. Yann LeCun called it "the coolest idea in machine learning in 20 years."
30.Going Deeper with Convolutions (GoogLeNet, Inception)
Szegedy et al., 2014
Showed clever architecture beats raw depth. Inception modules became a standard pattern.
31.Very Deep Convolutional Networks for Large-Scale Image Recognition (VGG)
Simonyan and Zisserman, 2014
Proved depth alone could help. Simple, hugely influential, easy to teach.
32.Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition (ResNet)
He, Zhang, Ren, Sun, 2015
Skip connections enabled 1000-plus-layer networks. The most cited paper of the 2010s.
33.Playing Atari with Deep Reinforcement Learning (DQN)
Mnih et al., 2013
Deep RL beat humans at Atari games. Started DeepMind's rise to prominence.
34.Mastering the Game of Go with Deep Neural Networks and Tree Search (AlphaGo)
Silver et al., 2016
Beat Lee Sedol 4 to 1. A cultural earthquake in Asia. Convinced governments that AI was real.
35.U-Net: Convolutional Networks for Biomedical Image Segmentation
Ronneberger, Fischer, Brox, 2015
Standard for biomedical segmentation. Underpins the architecture of every modern diffusion model.
36.Fast R-CNN
Girshick, 2015
Defined modern object detection.
37.You Only Look Once: Unified, Real-Time Object Detection (YOLO)
Redmon, Divvala, Girshick, Farhadi, 2015
Real-time object detection. Spawned an industry of computer-vision startups.
38.A Neural Algorithm of Artistic Style
Gatys, Ecker, Bethge, 2015
Neural style transfer. The first viral consumer AI moment.
39.FaceNet: A Unified Embedding for Face Recognition and Clustering
Schroff, Kalenichenko, Philbin, 2015
Face recognition at human level. Triplet loss became standard.
40.WaveNet: A Generative Model for Raw Audio
van den Oord et al., 2016
Raw audio generation. Powered Google Assistant voice.
41.Unsupervised Representation Learning with Deep Convolutional GANs (DCGAN)
Radford, Metz, Chintala, 2015
Made GANs actually trainable. Launched the generative-image wave.
42.Mask R-CNN
He, Gkioxari, Dollar, Girshick, 2017
Instance segmentation standard.

Era 5: Transformer Era 2017 to 2019

43.Attention Is All You Need
Vaswani et al., 2017
The transformer. Arguably the single most important paper of modern AI. Killed recurrent nets and birthed GPT, BERT, and everything since.
44.BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers for Language Understanding
Devlin, Chang, Lee, Toutanova, 2018
Bidirectional pretraining. Dominated NLP benchmarks overnight. Google Search runs on it.
45.Improving Language Understanding by Generative Pre-Training (GPT-1)
Radford, Narasimhan, Salimans, Sutskever, 2018
First GPT. Proved generative pretraining works for NLP.
46.Language Models are Unsupervised Multitask Learners (GPT-2)
Radford et al., 2019
1.5 billion parameters. OpenAI initially refused to release the model as "too dangerous." A marketing masterstroke.
47.Deep Contextualized Word Representations (ELMo)
Peters et al., 2018
Contextual embeddings. The bridge between Word2Vec and BERT.
48.Exploring the Limits of Transfer Learning with a Unified Text-to-Text Transformer (T5)
Raffel et al., 2019
Text-to-text framework. Unified all NLP tasks under one model.
49.RoBERTa: A Robustly Optimized BERT Pretraining Approach
Liu et al., 2019
Showed BERT was undertrained. Recipe matters as much as architecture.
50.Mastering the Game of Go Without Human Knowledge (AlphaGo Zero)
Silver et al., 2017
Mastered Go with zero human data. Pure self-play.
51.A General Reinforcement Learning Algorithm that Masters Chess, Shogi, and Go through Self-Play (AlphaZero)
Silver et al., 2017
Same algorithm beats chess, shogi, and Go. The generality moment.
52.Proximal Policy Optimization Algorithms (PPO)
Schulman et al., 2017
The reinforcement-learning workhorse. Powers RLHF.
53.The Lottery Ticket Hypothesis: Finding Sparse, Trainable Neural Networks
Frankle and Carbin, 2018
Sparse subnetworks exist that match full-network performance. Reshaped pruning research.
54.A Style-Based Generator Architecture for Generative Adversarial Networks (StyleGAN)
Karras, Laine, Aila, 2019
Photorealistic faces. "This Person Does Not Exist" became the public's first deepfake panic.

Era 6: Scale Era 2020 to 2022

55.Language Models are Few-Shot Learners (GPT-3)
Brown et al., 2020
175 billion parameters. In-context learning emerged. The "scale is all you need" moment that birthed the modern LLM industry.
56.Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models
Kaplan et al., 2020
Predictable loss versus compute curves. Made scale a science instead of a guess.
57.Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models (Chinchilla)
Hoffmann et al., 2022
GPT-3 was undertrained. Reshaped how every lab trains LLMs.
58.Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models (DDPM)
Ho, Jain, Abbeel, 2020
Made diffusion practical. The foundation of every modern image model.
59.Learning Transferable Visual Models From Natural Language Supervision (CLIP)
Radford et al., 2021
Joint image-text embedding. Powers DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, and modern image search.
60.Zero-Shot Text-to-Image Generation (DALL-E)
Ramesh et al., 2021
Text-to-image at scale. The public's first jaw-drop at generative AI.
61.High-Resolution Image Synthesis with Latent Diffusion Models (Stable Diffusion)
Rombach et al., 2022
Open-source DALL-E. Democratized image generation overnight.
62.Evaluating Large Language Models Trained on Code (Codex)
Chen et al., 2021
Powered GitHub Copilot. The first "AI does my job" moment for software engineers.
63.Highly Accurate Protein Structure Prediction with AlphaFold
Jumper et al., 2021
Solved a 50-year biology problem. Nobel-prize-worthy. Legitimized AI for science.
64.An Image is Worth 16x16 Words: Transformers for Image Recognition at Scale (ViT)
Dosovitskiy et al., 2020
Transformer beats CNNs at vision. Unified architectures across modalities.
65.Masked Autoencoders Are Scalable Vision Learners (MAE)
He et al., 2021
Self-supervised vision at scale. Vision's BERT moment.
66.Training Language Models to Follow Instructions with Human Feedback (InstructGPT)
Ouyang et al., 2022
RLHF. Made GPT actually useful. The direct precursor of ChatGPT.
67.Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models
Wei et al., 2022
"Think step by step." Free reasoning. Started prompt engineering as a discipline.
68.PaLM: Scaling Language Modeling with Pathways
Chowdhery et al., 2022
540 billion parameters. Google's scale flag-plant.
69.Flamingo: A Visual Language Model for Few-Shot Learning
Alayrac et al., 2022
Few-shot multimodal. Set the template for GPT-4V and modern vision-language models.
70.Robust Speech Recognition via Large-Scale Weak Supervision (Whisper)
Radford et al., 2022
Open-source ASR at human level. Killed Otter, Rev, and a generation of transcription startups overnight.
71.Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks (RAG)
Lewis et al., 2020
Combine retrieval and LLM. Powers most enterprise AI today.
72.Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback
Bai et al., 2022
Anthropic. RLAIF (AI feedback in place of human feedback). The technique behind Claude.

Era 7: Modern Frontier 2023 to 2026

73.ChatGPT Launch and System Card
OpenAI, 2022 to 2023
Not a research paper but a moment. 100 million users in 2 months. The fastest consumer-tech adoption in history.
74.GPT-4 Technical Report
OpenAI, 2023
Multimodal. No architecture details revealed. Marked the closed-frontier era.
75.LLaMA: Open and Efficient Foundation Language Models
Touvron et al., 2023
Open weights at frontier-adjacent quality. The Meta versus OpenAI war began here.
76.LLaMA 2: Open Foundation and Fine-Tuned Chat Models
Touvron et al., 2023
Commercially usable, RLHF in the open. Sparked the open-source LLM ecosystem.
77.Mistral 7B
Jiang et al., 2023
Small models punching far above their weight. Europe's first frontier-AI moment.
78.Mixtral of Experts (Mixtral 8x7B)
Jiang et al., 2024
Open Mixture-of-Experts. Sparse compute went mainstream.
79.Mamba: Linear-Time Sequence Modeling with Selective State Spaces
Gu and Dao, 2023
A real challenger to transformers. State-space models went mainstream.
80.Direct Preference Optimization (DPO)
Rafailov et al., 2023
RLHF without RL. Simpler, often better. Largely replaced PPO for alignment.
81.Toolformer: Language Models Can Teach Themselves to Use Tools
Schick et al., 2023
LLMs self-learn to use tools. Birthed the agents era.
82.ReAct: Synergizing Reasoning and Acting in Language Models
Yao et al., 2022
Think-then-act loop. The agent-loop template every framework now uses.
83.Tree of Thoughts: Deliberate Problem Solving with Large Language Models
Yao et al., 2023
Search over reasoning trees. Foundation for o1-style reasoning models.
84.Learning to Reason with LLMs (o1)
OpenAI, 2024
Test-time compute. Reasoning models became a distinct product category.
85.DeepSeek-R1: Incentivizing Reasoning Capability in LLMs via Reinforcement Learning
DeepSeek, 2025
Open reasoning model from China. RL-from-scratch worked. Wiped 600 billion dollars off Nvidia in one day.
86.Sora Technical Report
OpenAI, 2024
Video diffusion transformers. Hollywood-panic moment.
87.Video Generation Models as World Simulators
Brooks et al., 2024
Frames video models as physics simulators. Opened a new research direction.
88.A Generalist Agent (Gato)
Reed et al., 2022
One model, 600 tasks. The generalist-agent thesis stated plainly.
89.Voyager: An Open-Ended Embodied Agent with Large Language Models
Wang et al., 2023
LLM lives in Minecraft, learns skills. Embodied-agent proof-of-concept.
90.SWE-bench: Can Language Models Resolve Real-World GitHub Issues?
Jimenez et al., 2023
Benchmark for AI fixing real GitHub issues. Defined modern agent evaluation.
91.Scaling Monosemanticity / Towards Monosemanticity
Anthropic, 2024
Mechanistic interpretability at scale. We can finally see what features LLMs learn internally.
92.In-Context Learning and Induction Heads
Olsson et al., 2022
Discovered the circuit behind in-context learning. Interpretability landmark.
93.Sleeper Agents: Training Deceptive LLMs that Persist Through Safety Training
Anthropic, 2024
Models can hide misaligned behavior through training. Alignment alarm bell.
94.Weak-to-Strong Generalization
OpenAI, 2023
Can weak supervisors align stronger models? Foundation of the superalignment agenda.
95.Gemini 1.5: Unlocking Multimodal Understanding Across Millions of Tokens of Context
Google DeepMind, 2024
1 million plus token context. The long-context era began.
96.The Claude 3 Model Family
Anthropic, 2024
Constitutional AI at frontier scale. Vibes-based benchmarks (taste) became a thing.
97.The Llama 3 Herd of Models
Meta, 2024
70B and 405B parameters. Open weights at GPT-4 class. Cemented the open-frontier movement.
98.Grok and the Real-Time Data Moat
xAI, 2024
Real-time training on X data. Articulated the new data-moat thesis.
99.AlphaProof and AlphaGeometry 2: Olympiad-Level Mathematical Reasoning
DeepMind, 2024
Olympic-level math via neuro-symbolic reasoning. AI does formal proofs.
100.The Current Frontier (rotating slot)
Updated quarterly
Reserved for the most recent landmark when a user reaches the top of the canon. Updates four times a year. Marketing line: the canon is alive. Subscribers always finish at the actual frontier.
